# Summary

The Museum Studies curriculum is a **complete education in Museum Studies** using online materials.

The program emphasizes interdisciplinary foundations (drawing from history, art history, anthropology, and cultural studies), core museum practices, ethical and theoretical frameworks, and practical synthesis. It balances theory, historical context, professional skills, and critical reflection.

Note: When there are courses or books that don't fit into the curriculum but are otherwise of high quality,
they belong in [extras/courses](extras/courses.md), [extras/readings](extras/readings.md) or [extras/other_curricula](extras/other_curricula.md).

**Process**. Students can work through the curriculum alone or in groups, in order or out of order.

- We recommend doing all courses in **Core**, only skipping a course when you are certain that you've already learned the material previously.
- For simplicity, we recommend working through courses (especially Core) in order from top to bottom. Some students choose to study multiple courses at a time in order to vary the material they are working on in a day/week.
- The courses in the Advanced section are electives. Choose one track to specialize in and complete all the courses listed under it.

# Curriculum

## Intro Museum Studies

Build foundational knowledge in cultural heritage, history, and visual/material culture to understand the context of museums.  

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Introduction to Art History | Provides essential understanding of visual culture, artistic movements, and object interpretation central to many museums. | [*Gardner's Art Through the Ages*](https://www.amazon.com/Gardners-Art-Through-Ages-Global/dp/1285754999) | [Introduction to Art History (MIT OpenCourseWare)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/4-601-introduction-to-art-history-fall-2018/) |
| Introduction to Cultural Anthropology | Explores human cultures, material objects, and social contexts, key to ethnographic and cultural museums. | [*Cultural Anthropology: A Toolkit for a Global Age*](https://www.amazon.com/Cultural-Anthropology-Toolkit-Global-Age/dp/0393420124) | [Introduction to Anthropology](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/21a-00-introduction-to-anthropology-spring-2022/) |
| World History Survey | Offers broad historical context for collections, exhibitions, and the evolution of cultural institutions. | [*Traditions & Encounters: A Global Perspective on the Past*](https://archive.org/details/traditionsencoun0000bent_q6a8) | [History of the World to 1500 CE (Columbia University Lectures)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLBDA2E52FB1EF80C9) |
| Material Culture Studies | Introduces the analysis of objects as cultural artifacts, foundational for collections and exhibitions. | [*Material Culture Studies in America*](https://www.amazon.com/Material-Culture-Studies-America-Schlereth/dp/0761991603) | [An Introduction to Material Culture (OpenLearn)](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/history-the-arts/visual-art/an-introduction-material-culture/content-section-0) |

## Core Museum Studies

Master the history, theory, and operational practices of museums, equivalent to the bulk of an undergraduate major.  

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| History of Museums | Traces the evolution of museums from private cabinets to public institutions, understanding their societal role. | [*Museums in Motion*](https://archive.org/details/museumsinmotioni0002alex) | [Behind the Scenes at the 21st Century Museum (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/museum) |
| Museum Theory and Practice | Examines philosophical foundations, missions, and contemporary debates in museology. | [*Museum Studies: An Anthology of Contexts*](https://www.amazon.com/Museum-Studies-Anthology-Contexts-Carbonell/dp/0631228306) | Covered in [Behind the Scenes at the 21st Century Museum (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/museum) |
| Collections Management | Covers acquisition, documentation, storage, and care of objects, the backbone of museum operations. | [*Things Great and Small: Collections Management Policies*](https://www.amazon.com/Things-Great-Small-Collections-Management/dp/1933253037) | [Collections Management for Smaller Cultural Institutions (Connecting to Collections Care)](https://connectingtocollections.org/course-collections-management/) |
| Preventive Conservation | Focuses on preserving collections through environmental control, handling, and risk management. | [*Museum Registration Methods*](https://www.amazon.com/Museum-Registration-Methods-6th-Simmons/dp/1538113112) | Covered in [Collections Management for Smaller Cultural Institutions (Connecting to Collections Care)](https://connectingtocollections.org/course-collections-management/) |
| Exhibition Design and Development | Teaches planning, design, interpretation, and installation of exhibits for public engagement. | [*The Engaging Museum*](https://www.amazon.com/Engaging-Museum-Developing-Visitor-Involvement/dp/041534557X) | [Tangible Things: Discovering History Through Artworks, Artifacts, Scientific Specimens, and the Stuff Around You (Harvard/edX)](https://www.edx.org/learn/art-history/harvard-university-tangible-things-discovering-history-through-artworks-artifacts-scientific-specimens-and-the-stuff-around-you) |
| Museum Education and Public Programming | Explores learning theories, audience engagement, and educational roles of museums. | [*The Museum Educator's Manual*](https://www.amazon.com/Museum-Educators-Manual-Techniques-Johnson/dp/1442279052) | [Using Museums to Develop Learning (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/museum-learning) |
| Museum Management and Administration | Addresses governance, finance, marketing, and leadership in cultural institutions. | [*Museum Management and Marketing*](https://www.amazon.com/Museum-Management-Marketing-Richard-Sandell/dp/0415396298) | [The Cycle: Management of Successful Arts and Cultural Organizations (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/the-cycle) |

## Capstone

**Project Options** (Choose one):

- Design a full exhibition proposal (concept, object list, layout, interpretive text, budget).
- Develop a collections management policy or digital curation plan for a hypothetical museum.
- Write a research paper on a contemporary museum issue (e.g., repatriation case study) with practical recommendations.
- Create an educational program or visitor evaluation framework.

**Recommended Supporting Book**: [*Creating Exhibitions: Collaboration in the Planning, Development, and Design of Innovative Experiences* by Polly McKenna-Cress and Janet A. Kamien](https://www.amazon.com/Creating-Exhibitions-Collaboration-Development-Experiences/dp/1118306340)
